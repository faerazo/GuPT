## Summary

You study the development and structure in historic-cultural and contemporary context of the language. The course components are: language history and language change and grammar.

## Prerequisites and selection

### Entry requirements

The requirement for admission to the course is at least 22.5 credits from the Introductory course in English, 30 higher education credits or the equivalent thereof. Academic English in speech and writing (or written language proficiency) in the Introductory course or IELTS 7.0, no part below 6.0, or TOEFL 600, TWE results 5.0 should be included.

Entrance requirements valid from the autumn term 2025:

To be admissible to the course, students must have completed the English linguistics 7.5 credits, in the Introductory course in English, 30 credits or the equivalent thereof.

### Selection

Selection is based upon the number of credits from previous university studies, maximum 165 credits.
